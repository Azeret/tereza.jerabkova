Bridging Cosmic Scales: Understanding Star Formation from Molecular Clouds to Galactic Structures

How and what stars form is at the nexus of modern astrophysics: It sets the cosmic baryonic cycle, the production of chemical elements in the Universe, affects the formation and evolution of galaxies, and is important even on the smallest scales of the formation of planets and dust. The complex multi-scale nature of star formation leaves several fundamental questions asked in reviews decades ago (e.g., Shu, Adams, and Lizano 1987, Miller and Scalo 1979) still open for discussion today.

What are the initial conditions of star formation? Do stars form the same way in the Milky Way and in high-redshift galaxies? What is the role of dynamical processes in star clusters on the stellar populations in galaxies?
