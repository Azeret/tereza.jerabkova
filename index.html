Stellar Populations in Galaxies

About Us

Our group studies stellar populations both near and far, aiming to understand star and star-cluster formation using the best available data from the Milky Way. We apply this local approach to build stellar population models and chemical evolution studies for distant galaxies.

We are driven by fundamental questions such as:

  • Do stars form the same way in the Milky Way as they do at high redshift, where physical conditions are vastly different?
  • How do star clusters and other stellar structures dissolve and populate the Galactic field population?

The group is funded by the MUNI Award in Science and Humanities, under the project "Bridging Cosmic Scales: Understanding Star Formation from Molecular Clouds to Galactic Structures."
